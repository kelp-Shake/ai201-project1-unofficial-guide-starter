- by [unknown](#) **&#x21C5; 20**
  <br/> Some quick advice for becoming an DS or DA.

  - Learn either GCP or AWS, and master it.
  - Master SQL on either platform (BigQuery or MS SQL). It’s incredibly important as a DS/DA to know SQL at an expert level and there’s no way around it.
  - Analytics tools like Looker, Amplitude, Segment, Fivetran are all really good to know and look great on a resume if you can back them with skills.
  - If you want to be a DS specifically, knowing machine learning theory and in particular knowing both frequentist + Bayesian methods is powerful. MLOps is also important but comes second to knowing best practices for modeling and statistical tests (A/B and Diff in Diff are huge).

They unfortunately don’t teach you most of the best analytics tools in college and leave you to learn it on the job. Getting in early and learning these ahead of time puts you so far ahead. I honestly think UMD should put more SQL into their courses overall.
